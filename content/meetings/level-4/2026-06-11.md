# Level 4 Youth Day Design Sync

## Overview

Level 4 is the **slower, dwelling floor** — designed for deeper conversations and reflection rather than activity. The theme is *Facing Reality*: going beyond surface-level discussions about youth challenges.

The main constraint is the existing Skip infrastructure, which makes the entire floor orange. The plan is to **layer over it** with dark brown and soft blue accents using rugs, drapery, and hanging elements. The overall aesthetic is Nordic and warm, with mirrors used throughout for both decoration and photo opportunities.

Budget: **$5–6k** for the entire level.

---

## Spaces

### Entrance

- Decorates the escalator arrival from Level 3
- Offers a sneak peek into the Living Archive area to set the tone
- Sample stories on display to introduce the theme
- **Blackout poetry concept**: old books with permanent markers — visitors cross out words to surface hidden text

### Reality Check

- Zone focused on adulting: expectations vs. reality
- Two vendor partnerships in progress:
  - **Anyhow PlayPlay** — interactive exhibitions (quoted $10–20k, over budget)
  - **Playnting** — freedom of expression and crafting activities
- Mirrors throughout the space — whiteboard markers preferred over stickers (stickers damaged mirrors previously); drapery around mirrors possible but screen printing is expensive
- Signage strategy: A3 posters on easels borrowed from Crafter's Paradise

### Pause

- Multiple small studios for rest and disconnection at different intensities
- Lighting: lights off, floor lamps and ambient sources only; ripple lights from Shopee for calming effect
- Himalayan salt lamps to enhance the zen atmosphere
- Curtain installation challenge: no existing ceiling tracks — shower rod solutions being explored

### What We Carry

- Zone for taboo subjects: grief, social anxiety
- **NTU ADM student partnerships**:
  - Grief-focused square artworks for wall display
  - Social anxiety illustration books as conversation prompts
- Seating: wooden crates instead of coffee tables
- Conversation prompt cards (similar to *We're Not Really Strangers*)
- Brown paper table covers for direct writing
- **Stamp collection activity**: visitors receive a different stamp after each conversation; stamps combine into a complete image

### The Living Archive

- Main feature: a hanging clothesline display of people's stories
- Pre-event on **June 20–21** to collect initial stories before Youth Day
- Materials:
  - *Leave Your Message* vintage phone booth for video recordings
  - Clipboards for written stories (considering recycled/cardboard options)
  - Story cards designed like newspapers with blank story sections
  - Clothesline and pegs for hanging submissions
- Design coordination with **Adi** needed for a cohesive newspaper theme across the space

### Slow Growth

- Partnership with **Groove** and **City Sprouts**
- Focus on horticulture therapy and mindfulness activities
- Chalkboard signage to match the plant and growth theme
- Located next to the Living Archive — aesthetic consistency required between the two zones

### The Space Between

- Large studio (276 sq ft) for creative rest activities
- Youth community partnerships supplying art materials
- Mindfulness colouring activities
- Projector setup for calming ambiance (friend's projector available for testing)
- Signage needed to introduce community partners
